The head of a UK Ministerial Department is called the Secretary of State. All Ministers listed below belong to the current Labour Party government (as of December 2025).
| Department of State | Secretary of State (Minister) | Core Responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| Cabinet Office | Sir Keir Starmer (Prime Minister) | Supports the Prime Minister and ensures the effective running of government; central coordination, security, and integrity. |
| HM Treasury | Rachel Reeves, MP (Chancellor of the Exchequer) | Economic and financial policy, public spending, and tax. |
| Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) | Yvette Cooper, MP | Foreign policy, international development, intelligence, and promoting UK interests overseas. |
| Home Office | Shabana Mahmood, MP | Immigration, security, counter-terrorism, policing, and crime. |
| Ministry of Defence (MoD) | (Name not explicitly in search results, often changes with reshuffles) | National defence, armed forces, and procurement. |
| Ministry of Justice (MoJ) | Shabana Mahmood, MP (Lord Chancellor) | Oversees the justice system, courts, prisons, and constitutional affairs. |
| Department of Health and Social Care (DHSC) | Wes Streeting, MP | Health policy, the National Health Service (NHS), and adult social care in England. |
| Department for Education (DfE) | (Name not explicitly in search results) | Education policy for children and adults, children's services, and apprenticeships. |
| Department for Business and Trade (DBT) | Peter Kyle, MP | Business, trade, investment, export promotion, and industrial strategy. |
| Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) | Pat McFadden, MP | Welfare, pensions, and child maintenance policy. |
| Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ) | Ed Miliband, MP | Energy supply, climate change, and achieving Net Zero targets. |
| Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs (DEFRA) | Emma Reynolds, MP | Environmental protection, agriculture, fisheries, and rural affairs. |
